## 1.Introduction
SrFe12O19 is a [[ferromagnetic]] material that finds a wide range of industrial and commercial applications due to its stability, high Curie temperature, and cost-effectiveness. Modern equipment across various fields, including medical equipment, motors, transportation, [[aerospace]], communications, and [[instrumentation]], demands high performance, lightweight design, efficiency, and energy benefits. To reduce the performance gap between neodymiumbased metal and rare-earth-free ferrite materials, it is necessary to enhance the maximum magnetic energy product (BH)max of permanent magnet ferrite. The coercivity (Hcj) and saturation magnetization (Ms) are the key factors in determining the maximum magnetic energy product. Enhancing the Ms allows the material to provide a higher magnetic flux density within the same volume, thereby increasing the (BH)max and improving energy conversion efficiency. In high-performance permanent magnet applications, such as electric vehicle drive motors, magnetic levitation technology, and high-frequency electronic devices, the Ms of magnetic materials directly influences system output power and stability.1 Therefore, optimizing the Ms while maintaining high Hcj has become a crucial research focus to develop high-performance permanent magnet ferrites and break through their performance limitations.
